Task_1 lesson04(Pie chart)

Pie chart

  • Don’t describe each country separately

  • Compare countries and years together

  • Mention all xx categories

  • Detail 1 & Detail 2 should show the same thing like overview but more detail(using some data/ number)

  • Divide paragraph use categories

Introduction: 改写

e.g. The charts below show household spending patterns in two countries between 1980 and 2008.

改写为:The pie charts compare five categories of household expenditure(开支 ) in the UK and New Zealand in the years 1980 and 2008.

Overview 2 sentences, 2 main points

(找相同的趋势)It ‘s noticeable that the proportion of spending on food and drink ==fell== in both countries over the 28-year period, ==while== spending on the utility bills ==rose==. Also, ==(换主语)UK residents== spent a significantly large percentage of their ==household budgets(同义替换掉spending)== on leisure than their New Zealand ==counterparts(对应)==.

Details_1 (The same information as the overview but more details)

In 1980, 29% of an average New Zealand household budget ==went on== food and drink, while the equivalent figure for a UK home was 23%.(背诵). By 2008, expenditure(支出,替换spending) on food and drink had fallen by 4% in New Zealand, ==and by a full 10% in the UK==.(表示减小的百分比的类比). By contrast, ==both countries(换主语)== saw on increase in expenditure on utility bills for the average home, from 27% to 31% in New Zealand and from 26% to 28% in the UK.

Details_2

Leisure activities ==accounted for== the highest(No.1) proportion of UK household spending in both years, but only the third highest(No.3) proportion in New Zealand. In fact, in 2008, New Zealand only half as much in relative term on recreation==(休闲,同义替换leisure)==(17%) as UK resident(34%). In both countries, transport costs and other costs took roughly 15% and 10% of household budget respectively.

  • counterparts(对应)

    UK residents== spent a significantly large percentage of their household budgets(同义替换掉spending) on leisure than their New Zealand counterparts(对应).

  • respectively(分别地，用于两者或两者以上数据对比陈述)

    In both countries, transport costs and other costs took roughly 15% and 10% of household budget respectively

  • 29% of an average New Zealand household budget went on food and drink, while the equivalent figure for a UK home was 23%(两个不同的国家的相同的数据之间的比较，compare the same categories with different countries)
